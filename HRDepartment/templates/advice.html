Medical Advice

The relationship between the physician and his patient varies according to the type of illness or complaint, the concomitant severity of the illness and the treatment setting. With sud- den severe illness or injury where emergency treatment is called for, the patient makes few, if any, conscious decisions regarding his treatment and is under the complete control of the physician during the initial process. In less extreme cases, the patient comes to the physician voluntarily and often makes decisions regarding the degree of ac- ceptance or rejection of the physician's advice. A third situation is typical of many chronic illnesses. The patient himself carries out much of the treatment along with periodic consultations with the physician. Szasz and Hollender aptly term these three types of physician-patient relationships activity-passivity, guidance-cooperation and mutual participation.
